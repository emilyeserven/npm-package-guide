<SectionIntro>
Auth in the TypeScript ecosystem has been a persistent pain point. Auth.js (formerly NextAuth) is tightly coupled to Next.js, Clerk is a paid SaaS where you don't own the data, and rolling your own is a massive time sink. BetterAuth hits a sweet spot: open-source, self-hosted, framework-agnostic, and genuinely comprehensive.
</SectionIntro>

<SectionSubheading id="toc-problem">The auth problem in TypeScript</SectionSubheading>

Most TypeScript auth solutions force you into one of three trade-offs: framework lock-in (Auth.js), vendor lock-in (Clerk), or building from scratch. BetterAuth was designed to avoid all three.

<SectionSubheading id="toc-advantages">Core advantages</SectionSubheading>

<DefinitionTable>
  <DefRow term="You own the data">Everything lives in your database. No vendor lock-in, no data residency concerns. If you need to meet strict compliance requirements (government, healthcare, finance), this is a huge deal.</DefRow>
  <DefRow term="Framework agnostic from day one">BetterAuth wasn't built for Next.js and then ported — it was framework-agnostic from day one. Hono, Express, Fastify, Elysia, TanStack Start, SvelteKit, Astro — all first-class citizens.</DefRow>
  <DefRow term="Enterprise features aren't bolted on">SSO, SAML, SCIM, multi-tenancy, and RBAC are official plugins, not third-party hacks. You can go from a side project to enterprise-grade without swapping auth libraries.</DefRow>
  <DefRow term="Outstanding DX">Automatic schema generation, a CLI that handles migrations, type-safe client hooks, and built-in AI tooling (MCP server, Claude Code skills) mean you spend less time on auth plumbing.</DefRow>
</DefinitionTable>
