<SectionIntro>
Every partition is replicated across multiple brokers. One replica is the **leader** (handles reads/writes), the others are **followers** that stay in sync. If the leader fails, one of the in-sync replicas (ISRs) becomes the new leader automatically.
</SectionIntro>

<SectionSubheading id="toc-how-replication-works">How Replication Works</SectionSubheading>

When a producer writes to a partition, the record first goes to the leader broker. Followers continuously fetch new records from the leader and append them to their own log. Once all in-sync replicas have the record, it is considered "committed."

<DefinitionTable>
  <DefRow term="Replication factor">The number of copies of each partition across the cluster. A factor of 3 means each partition exists on 3 brokers.</DefRow>
  <DefRow term="Leader">The single broker that handles all reads and writes for a partition.</DefRow>
  <DefRow term="Follower">A broker that maintains a copy of the partition data by replicating from the leader.</DefRow>
</DefinitionTable>

<SectionSubheading id="toc-isr">In-Sync Replicas (ISR)</SectionSubheading>

The ISR set is the subset of replicas that are fully caught up with the leader. Kafka only considers a record committed once all ISR members have it. If a follower falls too far behind, it's removed from the ISR set until it catches up.

<Explainer>
With `acks=all` and a replication factor of 3, your data survives any 2 broker failures. This is the standard production configuration for critical data.
</Explainer>
