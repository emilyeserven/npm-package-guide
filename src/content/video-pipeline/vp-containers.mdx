<SectionSubheading id="toc-codecs">Codecs: The Compression Algorithm</SectionSubheading>

A **codec** (coder-decoder) is the algorithm that compresses and decompresses video or audio data. It determines *how* the pixels and audio samples are encoded into a smaller representation. Without codecs, video files would be absurdly large â€” a 2-hour 1080p movie would be over 1 terabyte uncompressed.

<SectionSubheading id="toc-containers">Containers: The Wrapper</SectionSubheading>

A **container** (sometimes called a wrapper or mux format) is the file format that holds the encoded video, audio, subtitles, metadata, and chapter markers together. Think of the codec as the language the content is written in, and the container as the book binding that holds the pages together.
