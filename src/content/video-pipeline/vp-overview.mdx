<SectionIntro>
Every time you watch a video online, an invisible pipeline has already processed, compressed, split, and delivered that content to you. Understanding this pipeline is the foundation for everything else in this guide.
</SectionIntro>

<SectionSubheading id="toc-pipeline">The Pipeline</SectionSubheading>

Click any stage below to understand what happens at each step of the video delivery journey:

<VideoPipelineDiagram />

The pipeline transforms raw camera footage into something that can be streamed smoothly over the internet. Each stage has a specific job:

<SectionList>
<ColItem>**Capture** records the raw video and wraps it in an initial container format.</ColItem>
<ColItem>**Inspect** reads the file's metadata to understand its codec, resolution, and bitrate.</ColItem>
<ColItem>**Transcode** re-encodes the video into multiple quality levels optimized for different devices and networks.</ColItem>
<ColItem>**Package** segments each quality level into small chunks with manifest playlists.</ColItem>
<ColItem>**Deliver** pushes the segments to a CDN for global, low-latency access.</ColItem>
<ColItem>**Play** uses adaptive bitrate algorithms to switch quality seamlessly during playback.</ColItem>
</SectionList>

<SectionSubheading id="toc-why">Why It Matters</SectionSubheading>

Understanding the pipeline helps you make informed decisions about video infrastructure. Without this knowledge, you might serve a single massive file that buffers constantly, choose the wrong codec for your audience, or skip critical optimizations like adaptive bitrate streaming.
