<SectionIntro>
Before diving deeper into EKS, you need a mental model of Kubernetes itself. Kubernetes (often called K8s) is a container orchestration platform. It decides *where* your containers run, *restarts* them if they crash, *scales* them up/down, and handles *networking* between them.
</SectionIntro>

<SectionSubheading id="toc-intro">{'\u{1F4A1}'} Why Kubernetes?</SectionSubheading>

Kubernetes manages WHERE your containers run, HOW MANY copies, WHEN to restart them, and HOW traffic reaches them. It's the standard for container orchestration in production environments.
