<SectionIntro>
Navigation is how users **move** through an information space and **orient** themselves within it. Good navigation answers three questions at every point in the experience.
</SectionIntro>

<SectionSubheading id="toc-three-questions">The Three Questions</SectionSubheading>

At any point in a website, application, or document, users need answers to:

<DefinitionTable>
  <DefRow term="Where am I?" definition="Breadcrumbs, highlighted nav items, page titles, and visual context tell users their current location." />
  <DefRow term="Where can I go?" definition="Visible navigation options, links, and calls to action show available destinations." />
  <DefRow term="How do I get back?" definition="Back buttons, breadcrumbs, logo links, and browser history provide escape routes." />
</DefinitionTable>

If your interface can't answer all three at a glance, users feel lost â€” and lost users leave.

A well-designed application uses multiple navigation types simultaneously. This guide, for example, combines **global** (top-level guide list), **local** (sidebar within the guide), **contextual** (inline NavLinks between pages), **supplementary** (command palette search), and **courtesy** (breadcrumbs, prev/next buttons) navigation.

<SectionSubheading id="toc-wayfinding">Wayfinding Cues</SectionSubheading>

Wayfinding is the set of environmental cues that help people orient themselves â€” a concept borrowed from physical architecture.

In **physical spaces**, wayfinding cues include floor numbers, room signs, color-coded zones, and "You Are Here" maps. In **digital spaces**, the equivalents are:

<DefinitionTable>
  <DefRow term="Active state indicators" definition="Highlighted sidebar items, bolded breadcrumbs, underlined tabs â€” showing the current location." />
  <DefRow term="Visual hierarchy" definition="Larger headings, indented sub-items, grouped sections â€” showing relationships between content." />
  <DefRow term="Progress indicators" definition="Step counters, progress bars, section completion â€” showing how far along the user is." />
  <DefRow term="Consistent positioning" definition="Navigation always in the same place, logos always link home â€” building muscle memory." />
</DefinitionTable>

<Gotcha>
Don't rely on color alone for wayfinding. About 8% of men have some form of color blindness. Always combine color with other cues: bold text, icons, position, or borders.
</Gotcha>
